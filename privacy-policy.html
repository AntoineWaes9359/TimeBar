Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2026

TimeBar (“we”, “our”, or “the app”) is a menubar application for macOS that displays multiple time zones. This policy describes how we handle information in connection with the app.

1. Data collection

TimeBar is designed to work locally on your Mac. We do not collect, transmit, or store your personal data on our servers. The app does not use analytics, advertising, or third‑party tracking.

2. Data stored on your device

The app stores the following data locally on your Mac (using standard system storage such as User Defaults) to provide the features you use:

  • Time zones and locations you add (e.g. name, flag/emoji, trigram, time zone identifier)
  • Your preferences (e.g. time format 12h/24h, work hours, menubar display style)

This data remains on your device and is not sent to us or any third party.

3. No account required

TimeBar does not require an account or sign‑in. There is no user profile or cloud sync.

4. Clipboard

When you use the “copy” feature, the app writes the selected time zone and times to your Mac’s clipboard. This is a local action only; we do not access or receive the contents of your clipboard.

5. Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will be revised when we do. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

6. Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy or TimeBar’s practices, you can contact us at the support or contact address provided in the App Store listing or in the app.
